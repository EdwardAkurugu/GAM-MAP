The <b>Generalised Additive Model-Based Malaria Analytics Platform (GAM-MAP)</b> analyses temporal and climatic drivers of uncomplicated malaria across Ghana's regions using <b>Generalised Additive Models (GAMs)</b>.

<div style="color:green; ">
What This App Does?
</div>

This interactive tool explores how climate variables influence malaria case patterns from 2012–2023 across regions in Ghana.

<div style="color:green; margin-top:10px;">
Key Features:
</div>

- Regional GAM analysis of climate-malaria relationships
- Temporal pattern exploration (trends, seasonality, time series decomposition)
- Model performance evaluation and diagnostics
- Evidence-based insights for malaria control strategies

<div style="color:green; margin-top:10px;">
Data Coverage
</div>
12 years (2012-2023) | 10 Regions | Monthly observations


<div style="color:green; margin-top:10px;">
Alignment with National Goals
</div>
The app supports <b>Ghana's National Malaria Elimination Programme (NMEP)</b> by providing climate-informed analytical tools for understanding the temporal and environmental drivers of malaria burden across Ghana's regions.
